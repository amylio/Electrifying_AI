Project Overview


The purpose of this project is to conduct an overall analysis of the electric vehicle car market, availability of charging stations, potential climate implications and use of Machine Learning to make future predictions on likelihood of EV ownership.

Electric vehicles currently make up 2% of all cars sold in the US. Many drivers have put off purchasing electric vehicles for many reasons, such as expense and concerns over the accessibility to charging options (e.g. public charging stations, in-home setup). However, current trends show that these statistics are likely to change as the automotive market moves to introduce more affordable models.

    • A global focus on reducing greenhouse gases in support of minimizing climate change.
    • Most automakers have committed to all of its sales to be zero-emission, electrified vehicles by 2040 with at least 40% of the sales by 2030. [Source]
    • The president’s current infrastructure plan plan intends to bring electric cars fully into the mainstream by spending $174 billion to promote EVs and build charging stations. [Source]

Using Machine Learning to Predict Ownership


We use Machine Learning to explore the relationship of various demographic variables with electric vehicle ownership to make future predictions on likelihood of EV Ownership. By finding key variables that contribute to whether someone is likely to own an electric vehicle and comparing those demographics to geographic data such as number of EV Charging Stations, we can propose to public policy makers where might be the next best markets for installing charging stations.

We approached this with two Machine Learning algorithms: Random Forest Classifier and Decision Tree. Both models achieved an accuracy score of .90. This means that 90% of observation in the testing set were predicted correctly by the model.

Features for the model: Demographic Data such as gender, employment, education, income, household size, etc.

Analysis of the Data


Data sourced from the 2019 California Vehicle Survey conducted by California Energy Commission showed the following:

* 63.9% of the owners are in the 35-64 age group



* There are more male owners than female owners and the majority of the owners have a college or post graduate degree with an average income greater than $100K




Summary of Findings


After reviewing all of the data that was collected and analyzing the results, we determined that there were several key variables that a potential owner took into consideration when they opted to purchase an Electric Vehicle. These variables are:

    • Overall cost of vehicle
    • Accessibility to charging station or in-home charging options
    • Size and capacity of vehicle (e.g. ability to fit families with multiple passengers including children)
    • Tax credits and incentives (e.g. toll free driving, carpool lane and parking access for single occupants, etc.)
By finding the key variables and comparing those demographics to geographic data such as number of EV Charging Stations, we can propose to public policy makers where might be the next best markets for installing charging stations.

All About Electric Vehicles

What is an Electric Vehicle?


According to Wikipedia [1] , "An electric vehicle (EV) is a vehicle that uses one or more electric motors or traction motors for propulsion. An electric vehicle may be powered through a collector system by electricity from off-vehicle sources, or may be self-contained with a battery, solar panels, fuel cells or an electric generator to convert fuel to electricity. EVs include, but are not limited to, road and rail vehicles, surface and underwater vessels, electric aircraft and electric spacecraft."

Quick History Lesson...


"Electric vehicles first appeared in the mid-19th century. An electric vehicle held the vehicular land speed record until around 1900. The high cost, low top speed, and short-range of battery electric vehicles, compared to 20th century internal combustion engine vehicles, led to a worldwide decline in their use as private motor vehicles; although electric vehicles have continued to be used in the form of loading and freight equipment and public transport – especially rail vehicles." [2]
